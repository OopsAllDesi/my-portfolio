Business

When I first started working with media business concepts (Level A), I was mainly following guidance and getting familiar with the basic tools and terms. As I moved to Level B, I began to work more independently—analyzing trends, thinking strategically, and applying business knowledge to realistic cases. By the time I reached Level C, I was able to confidently create well-supported advice and use business insights to help shape decision-making, just like in real business environments.

Level B

This document presents a concept I developed for Vice Media Group with the aim of increasing audience engagement and positioning their website as the primary platform for views. Level C

This document is a comprehensive business plan for Arkavika, a company I created that would help connect young creatives and media professionals, in order for the young people to create connections and find projects easily. Since the company was mine, I had to write the business plan from scratch while designing the company, unlike the previous times. I created the Mission and Vision, the KPIs, the Business Model Canvas I worked on the Porter’s Five Forces, the STP model, the T( technology) from PESTEL, the Marketing Mix, the 7 S model, the CRM practices, part of the Stakeholder Analysis and Risk analysis, the Marketing proposition and Marketing Mix

Research

Research started out as a guided process for me in Level A, where I learned the steps and structure while getting used to different methods. In Level B, I took more control by designing my own research questions, collecting data, and choosing the right approach for the topic. By Level C, I was confidently conducting independent research, critically analyzing results, and presenting everything in a professional way that could be used in actual creative business practice.
